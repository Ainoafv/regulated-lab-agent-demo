Can AI take work off a QC lab without ever releasing a batch on its own?

A working prototype I built to show how I'd automate and integrate AI into lab workflows: a scientist asks for help with batch B-2047, and the model can propose actions — but a permission policy in code decides what runs, what waits for a human, and what is never automated. Every step is logged and tamper-evident. Fictional scenario — not Thermo Fisher software.

Permission policy (enforced in code, not prompts):

auto summarize, query LIMS — read-only, low risk

ask flag anomaly, write ELN — human review before write

never release batch, change LIMS status — human only, GxP-critical

Unknown action → fail closed (never). AUTO needs confidence ≥ 0.70 or it escalates.

The compliance story: every action is logged, and each row is hashed to the one before it (prev_hash). Editing history breaks the chain — try Tamper then Verify. Designed around 21 CFR Part 11 and ALCOA++ data-integrity expectations.

Why I built this

I come from science and bioinformatics, and I now build and integrate AI systems end-to-end. This prototype is how I think about the future of the connected lab: automating the repetitive work (summaries, look-ups, draft notes), integrating across LIMS / ELN / instruments, and keeping a human in control of anything that touches the quality record — the same direction the industry is taking with QC-analyst agents, lab orchestration and responsible-AI governance. My edge isn't another LIMS CV — it's seeing the workflow from the end user's side (scientist, QA, IT) and being able to design, build and explain it to each of them.
